Greece

Incredibly beautiful country. I had a chance to spend 2 days in Rhodos and 3 months in Cos.
Rhodos is very nice, especially the Old Town. Although as in most of the touristic beaches you have to be careful with pickpockets and vendors around the beaches
Cos however, is even more wonderful. This small island has everything that you need for a week-long holiday. Especially if you like parties.
You can find a "Paradise" sand beach, have a bath in a natural thermal lake or have a boat trip to Turkey.

One of the richest historical cities of Europe. I think everybody should visit it at least once.
It is possible to walk around in one day. Of course you could spend some more days, if you want to get to know the culture and deepen the history a little more.
So have fun there! :)

Venice

Well, what could I tell you that you do not know already about Venice?
Pretty amazing little town. It is perfect to spend some romantic days there.
Try the gondolas and have a special Italian dinner on the beach of Adriatic Sea.
Especially wonderful in summer. Although you could visit it for the huge carneval in February.

Barcelona

You probably need a whole week in Barcelona to have time for a proper visit.
Barcelona is not a typical Spanish cultural city, but you can meet the Catalan culture.
Swim in Balearic Sea, see some unique buildings from Güell and Gaudi, (hopefully the reconstructions of Sagrada Familia will be done by then).
If you still have time, visit the Zoo, the Tibidabo and the amusement park.
Ohh, yeah and have fun!! :)

Dubrovnik

If you ever plan to visit Croatia, you just have to get to Dubrovnik as well.
Croatia is full of with amazing cities, like Zadar or Split,
not to mention the "natural miracle", the Plitvice Lakes Natural Park.
After seeing all these sites of Croatia, I can honestly tell you, Dubrovnik is the most beautiful part of it.
It is modern, but still historical, and the view of the Adriatic See is just breathtaking.

Tenerife

Tenerife is situated in the Gran Canarias.
It is a very nice island with typical Spanish atmosphere.
You can swim in the Atlantic Ocean, travel up to the Teide Vulcan, go for a boat trip to find dolphins and whales in the open water, visit the Zoo with different animal shows and have a circle to see all cities.
And the best thing is that you can travel even in winter, because the temperature still gooood.

Dubai

Why are so many people fascinated by Dubai?
Before I went there I was, too. But very soon I realised it is not that big a deal.
Well, it is perfect for those who love modern architecture and hot weather.
You can enjoy seeing huge hotels and skyscrapers in the middle of a desert,
swim in Persian Gulf, ski in a mall, and shop, shop, shop!
Have fun if you once get there! But be ready for heat of the sun!!

Berlin

To tell you the truth this is my home right now.
To be even more honest, first I had huge doubts about this city, not to mention the language.
I needed only one month to realise, this is a great city.
Well, there are not too many (nice) places to visit, although it is full of with museums, which is cool.
The feeling, that moves the whole city, is the acceptance.
The mentality and human behaviour that I see here every day, is wonderful.
And if you need a little more history and beauty, just travel to Potsdam.
It is not far and is definitely worth a day to visit.

Budapest

This is my personal favourite city.
Not just because I spent 6 years of my life there so far. But because it is especially breathtaking.
According to the European capital cities, it is quite cheap. And still you have a lot of opportunities.
You can have an original sightseeing tour, walk on Danube Bank and see almost everything, like the magnificent Buda Castle, Parliament, Liberty Statue on Gellért Hill, Chain Bridge....
Besides you can wander to the Danube Bend, have a drink in a ruin pub or go to Circus.
There is not even enough space here to tell you, how much I love this city.
